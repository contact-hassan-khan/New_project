Security & Non-Disclosure Policy

Last Updated: January 2024

1. Our Security Commitment

As a cybersecurity company, we maintain the highest security standards for our operations and client engagements. We are ISO 27001:2022 certified and follow industry best practices.

2. Client Data Protection

  • All client data is encrypted at rest and in transit
  • Access controls and need-to-know basis enforcement
  • Regular security audits and penetration testing of our infrastructure
  • Secure deletion of data after engagement completion
  • No data sharing with third parties without explicit consent

3. Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)

Cyber Elite automatically enters into NDAs with all clients and partners. Key provisions include:

  • Confidentiality of all shared information
  • Restriction on disclosure to third parties
  • Return or destruction of confidential materials post-engagement
  • Survival clause extending obligations beyond contract termination
  • Legal remedies for breach of confidentiality

4. Employee Confidentiality

All employees and contractors sign comprehensive NDAs and undergo security background checks. Regular security awareness training is mandatory.

5. Responsible Disclosure

For vulnerability findings during engagements, we follow responsible disclosure practices:

  • Immediate notification of critical findings
  • Detailed reports provided only to authorized personnel
  • No public disclosure without client consent
  • Remediation support provided

6. Compliance Standards

Our security practices comply with:

  • ISO 27001:2022 Information Security Management
  • CERT-In Guidelines
  • GDPR Data Protection Requirements
  • Industry-specific regulations (HIPAA, PCI-DSS as applicable)

7. Incident Response

In the unlikely event of a security incident affecting client data, we have a documented incident response plan including immediate notification, investigation, and remediation.
